\section{Lists}

A bulleted list is \texttt{itemize}; each entry starts with \verb|\item|:
\begin{itemize}
    \item First point.
    \item Second point.
    \item Lists can nest:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item A sub-point.
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

Swap \texttt{itemize} for \texttt{enumerate} to number them:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Step one.
    \item Step two.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Tables}

In a \texttt{tabular}, \verb|{l c r}| declares three columns --- left-,
centre-, and right-aligned. \verb|&| separates cells, \verb|\\| ends a
row, and \verb|\hline| draws a rule.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{l c r}
    \hline
    Item     & Quantity & Price \\
    \hline
    Notebook & 3        & 4.50  \\
    Pen      & 12       & 1.20  \\
    \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
